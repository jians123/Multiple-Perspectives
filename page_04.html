The Imperial Palace

The Forbidden City is also known as the Forbidden City. In ancient China, the planning concept of "harmony between man and nature" was emphasized, and the stars in the sky were used to correspond to the capital planning, so as to highlight the legitimacy of the regime and the supremacy of imperial power. The emperor of heaven lived in Ziwei Palace, while the emperor of the world claimed that he was the "Son of Heaven" who was ordered by heaven. His residence should be a symbol of Ziwei Palace to correspond to the emperor of heaven. The Book of the Later Han Dynasty stated that "Ziwei Palace is the residence of God. The king built a palace and acted like an elephant.". Ziwei, Ziyuan, Zigong, etc. became the nickname of the imperial palace. As the feudal imperial palace was a forbidden area in ancient times, which was inaccessible to ordinary people, it was called the "Forbidden City". In the early Ming Dynasty, together with the Forbidden City, they were collectively called the "Imperial City". About the middle and late Ming Dynasty, they were distinguished from the Forbidden City, that is, the palace city was called the "Forbidden City", and the Forbidden City was called the "Imperial City".
